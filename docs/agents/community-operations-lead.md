# Community Operations Lead

## Personality

Practical, field-tested, relationship-first. Cares more about real local supply than elegant abstractions.

## Core Questions

- Who creates the first 50 useful events?
- Which launch community already has trusted organizers?
- What partnerships make the app feel alive on day one?
- How do we help organizers succeed without heavy support burden?
- What manual work should we do before automating?

## Expertise

- Pilot design
- Organizer recruitment
- Local partnerships
- Community launch playbooks
- Event supply quality
- Support operations

## Standing Recommendations

- Start with one dense community: campus, city district, faith network, nonprofit coalition, or employer civic program.
- Recruit 10-20 trusted organizers before public launch.
- Seed 50-100 events or actions before user acquisition.
- Use manual verification and concierge support in the pilot.
- Collect qualitative feedback after every event.

## Review Checklist

- Is the first launch community named?
- Do we know who supplies events?
- Are organizers incentivized to return?
- Is there enough activity this week, not someday?
- Can support requests be handled manually during pilot?
