# Hand Theme Navigation Language

Status: working draft

Handprint can use hand-language as a warm product system: not cute for its own sake, but memorable, human, and tied to the idea that people change the world with what is within reach. The language should make the app feel active, relational, and hopeful without turning every screen into a pun.

This document is a parking lot and decision guide for future naming, page headers, rewards language, and UX copy.

## Current Direction

- Reach: find useful actions within reach.
- Print: see the Handprint your actions are leaving behind and move between World Changer and World Enabler identity.
- Wave: send your Handprint outward through QR, invitations, captions, and public share cards.
- Shake: connect in person, then grow a useful network of World Changers and World Enablers.
- Hi-Five: celebrate a verified badge or achievement with a lightweight animated reaction.
- Profile: identity and settings behind the profile icon.
- Review: a role-gated steward surface, outside primary member navigation.

## Naming Principles

1. Use hand-language for the most important branded concepts.
2. Keep utility labels plain when clarity matters more than brand flavor.
3. Prefer active language over passive status language.
4. Avoid joke-density. A few strong terms will feel ownable; too many will feel gimmicky.
5. Let the metaphor carry the mission: people use their hands to help, gather, build, invite, repair, lift, and affirm.

## Primary Navigation System

- Reach
- Print
- Wave
- Shake

This is the V1 narrative spine. Hi-Five is an interaction, Profile is an icon-level utility, World Enabler tools live inside Print, and Review remains role-gated. A fifth primary destination should be added only when a critical user journey cannot clearly belong to one of the four.

## Strong Candidate Labels

### Reach

Use for finding useful actions. The name ties location, ability, time, and personal agency to the central question: what can I change within reach of my hands?

Product uses:
- Search and location.
- Cause, skill, distance, reward, and event-type filters.
- Event results and details.
- RSVP and check-in.
- World Enabler and event discovery.

### Print

Use for the user's accumulated identity and progress. Print answers: what Handprint am I leaving behind?

Product uses:
- World Changer rank and progress.
- Marks, badges, credentials, goals, and rewards.
- Participation and impact history.
- World Enabler unlock path and role switch.
- The visual Handprint itself.

### Wave

Use for sharing, QR, outward invitation, social posts, and public identity cards.

Best headers:
- Send your Handprint outward.
- Wave someone in.
- Let others see what changed.

Recommendation: Wave is strong and brandable.

Product uses:
- QR card.
- Public Handprint link.
- Social share kit.
- Invite captions.
- Milestone announcements.
- Event recruitment posts.

### Shake

Use for in-person connection first, then the network created through those deliberate meetings.

Best headers:
- Your World Changing Network.
- People and groups within reach.
- Trusted hands nearby.

Recommendation: Shake should always open in listening mode so its purpose is felt before the saved network appears.

Product uses:
- Nearby connection and public identity exchange.
- World Changers and World Enablers met or deliberately added.
- Shared event networks.
- Trusted recruitment paths.
- People who have helped together.
- People and organizations near your interests.

### World Enabler Mode

World Enabler is a role inside Print, not a fifth top-level destination. It owns event creation, participant confirmation, impact receipts, World Enabler accolades, and organization identity.

### Review

Best current alternatives:
- Steward: best values fit for trust and verification.
- Verify: clearest function, less warm.
- Seal: good for approval/certification, too narrow for moderation and trust.

Recommendation: consider renaming Review to Steward once reviewer tools are more central.

## Supporting Product Language

- Handprint: the public record of useful action.
- Mark: a verified contribution.
- Wave: a share or outward invitation.
- Handshake: a trusted connection.
- Helping hand: entry-level skill or beginner-friendly role.
- Steady hands: reliability and repeat contribution.
- Many hands: group impact, event momentum, and World Enabler mobilization.
- Open hands: inclusive roles for new World Changers.
- Hands-on: action-oriented events that qualify for rewards.
- Hand-off: referral or transition to another World Changer/World Enabler.
- Lift: capacity-building support, rewards, scholarships, or microgrants.
- Palm: personal view of your identity, settings, or private dashboard.
- Reach: your practical radius of action and influence.
- Grip: commitment or retained engagement; use cautiously because it can feel forceful.
- High five: lightweight praise; use sparingly because it can feel juvenile if overused.

## Skills And Role Language

Entry-level skills should sound welcoming and immediately usable:

- Helping hand
- Willing hands
- Team spirit
- Friendly welcome
- Setup helper
- Cleanup helper
- Runner
- Learner
- Enthusiast
- Neighborly support

Earned or vetted skills should sound more trusted:

- Steady hands: reliability earned through repeated confirmed participation.
- Trusted driver: validated driving eligibility where required.
- Grant writer: verified through training, portfolio, or organizer approval.
- Mentor: earned through training, background checks where needed, and organizer trust.
- Crew lead: earned by consistent participation and positive host ratings.
- Safety lead: earned through approved safety, first aid, or preparedness credentials.

## Rewards Language

Reward language should reinforce that rewards expand impact, not just personal consumption.

- Expand Your Reach: umbrella phrase for rewards.
- Step Forward: footwear, gear, or mobility-related support.
- Goody Two Shoes: playful public award for high-walking, high-service contribution milestones.
- Lift Grant: microgrant that helps a user or team do more.
- Many Hands Award: group or team contribution reward.
- World Awareness Fellowship: travel or cultural learning reward tied to service and reflection.
- Helping Hand Scholarship: education support for consistent World Changers.
- Steady Hands Scholarship: higher-tier scholarship for reliability and verified impact.
- World Changer Scholarship: top-tier scholarship tied to leadership, impact, and character.

## Event And Verification Language

- Hands-on event: reward-eligible event that does something measurable.
- Awareness event: eligible for discovery and promotion, but not automatically reward-eligible.
- Action add-on: a practical do-ism opportunity attached to an awareness event.
- Impact receipt: organizer-confirmed summary of what happened.
- Mark earned: user-facing phrase for a confirmed contribution.
- Evidence: supporting proof, kept simple and affirming.
- Verified by: organizer or Handprint reviewer attribution.
- Within reach: location, timing, and personal fit signal.

## Tone Examples

Preferred:

- "You earned a new mark."
- "Wave this milestone to invite others in."
- "Add this World Enabler to your Shake network."
- "This action is hands-on and reward eligible."
- "Your reach grew by 25 verified points."

Avoid:

- "Exploit your network."
- "Farm points."
- "Boost engagement."
- "Viral growth hack."
- "Punish inactive users."

Internal-only language can be more operational, but user-facing copy should stay affirming, useful, and grounded in real-world action.

## Future Naming Parking Lot

Possible future modules:

- Open Hands: beginner onboarding and first-event discovery.
- Many Hands: group projects, team impact, and collective milestones.
- Steady Hands: reliability profile and trust signals.
- Hand-off: referrals, introductions, and role transfers.
- Lift: grants, rewards, scholarships, and capacity-building support.
- Palm: private user settings and identity controls.
- Reach Map: geographic view of useful action nearby.
- Markbook: ledger of verified marks and impact receipts.
- Good Hands Review: trust and safety reviewer workspace.
- Wave Kit: share templates and platform-specific export tools.

## Decision Notes

- Keep the four primary destinations stable: Reach, Print, Wave, and Shake.
- Keep Hi-Five as an achievement reaction, not a page.
- Keep World Enabler tools inside Print.
- Keep Profile behind the profile icon.
- Revisit "Review" to "Steward" after trust workflows are clearer and more visible.
- Do not add another primary destination unless the journey genuinely cannot fit the four-part narrative.

## Guardrail

Do not overuse hand puns in every label. The best system uses a few strong product nouns, then keeps supporting copy plain and useful.
