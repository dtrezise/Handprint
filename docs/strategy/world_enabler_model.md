# World Enabler Model - Working Proposal

Status: working draft

World Enablers are the people and organizations that create useful, affirming, verifiable opportunities for World Changers. They are not just event hosts. They expand the reach of other people's Handprints by turning good intentions into practical action, confirmed contribution, and durable community proof.

## Core Distinction

World Changer:
- Shows up.
- Builds a public record of contribution.
- Earns badges, points, skills, referrals, and rewards through verified action.
- Recruits others by sharing their Handprint and lived example.

World Enabler:
- Creates opportunities for others to show up.
- Confirms participation and impact.
- Designs events that satisfy the Do Something rubric.
- Builds a public World Enabler Handprint based on mobilization, trust, transparency, and community outcomes.

## Unlock Path

World Enabler access should not be open by default. A World Changer should unlock the ability to host after enough trusted participation.

Initial V1 proposal:
- Builder tier or higher.
- Repeated reward-eligible attendance.
- Positive confirmation history from existing World Enablers.
- Referral credibility from people who actually attend and contribute.
- No unresolved trust or safety flags.

Optional manual override:
- Handprint reviewers can invite known nonprofits, schools, churches, community groups, companies, or civic organizations into the World Enabler portal before an individual account earns the full unlock.

## Portal Functions

The World Enabler portal should eventually include:
- Event/action submission.
- Do Something rubric review.
- Role and skill needs.
- Badge and point preview.
- Attendance roster.
- Check-in confirmation.
- Post-event impact receipt creation.
- World Changer recommendations for hard-to-fill roles.
- Sponsor slot controls.
- Grant-ready proof and accolade history.

## World Enabler Rewards

World Enablers should have a different reward model than World Changers.

Potential reward categories:
- Mobilization: confirmed participants, repeat attendance, successful recruiting.
- Impact: verified outputs, beneficiary confirmation, durable community benefit.
- Trust: clean confirmation history, accurate claims, low dispute rates.
- Inclusion: meaningful roles for new World Changers, youth-safe pathways, accessibility.
- Stewardship: respectful sponsorships, low spam, good follow-through.

Example accolades:
- First Useful Action Hosted.
- Trusted Mobilizer.
- Newcomer Builder.
- Impact Receipt Verified.
- Sponsor Steward.
- Grant-Ready Enabler.

## Guardrails

World Enablers can create trust or damage it quickly, so the product should protect against:
- Inflated points.
- Vanity events that do not do anything.
- Overclaiming impact.
- Political or ideological bait-and-switch.
- Sponsor spam.
- Unsafe roles for minors or vulnerable people.
- Favoritism in badge or point issuance.
- Fake attendance confirmation.

V1 should keep point values rubric-controlled. World Enablers describe the event and confirm real participation; Handprint calculates or reviews reward value.

## Discovery Role

Following a World Enabler should help users discover:
- New events hosted by that Enabler.
- World Changers who participate with them.
- Similar Enablers nearby.
- Public QR Handprint pages for recruiting.
- Impact receipts and badges that prove the Enabler does what they claim.

Following a World Changer should help users discover:
- Events they are joining.
- Events they are recruiting for.
- World Enablers they trust.
- Similar opportunities in the same cause/skill graph.

## Areas To Explore

- What exact tier unlocks World Enabler access?
- Should organizational accounts be separate from individual accounts?
- Can a World Changer represent an organization, or should the organization own the Enabler page?
- How should Handprint handle schools, youth groups, churches, and companies?
- What makes a World Enabler eligible to issue badges?
- Which confirmations require human review?
- Can World Enablers lose future privileges without losing past accomplishments?
- How do sponsor funds support rewards without buying trust?
- How should World Enabler QR pages differ from World Changer QR pages?
- What is the minimum viable portal for V1 launch?

## V1 Product Posture

World Enablers should feel prestigious but service-oriented. The portal should not feel like an ad manager. It should feel like a stewardship console: create useful action, invite the right people, confirm what happened, publish proof, and expand the reach of everyone involved.

## Current Prototype Additions

- The Organize tab now shows a World Enabler unlock path before portal access.
- The unlocked portal preview frames the operating model as show up, recruit responsibly, learn the rubric, earn trust reviews, and open the portal.
- The Handshake tab now treats followed World Enablers and World Changers as a discovery network rather than a saved-list only.
- The Wave tab now includes QR reliability checks and export presets so Handprint identity can move between app, social, and physical settings while staying verifiable.

## Next Mechanics To Formalize

- Credibility score formula for World Enabler eligibility.
- Which organizations can be reviewer-invited before an individual earns the unlock.
- Badge issuance limits by event type and trust tier.
- How World Enabler-to-World Changer recruiting messages become official invites.
- How sponsor-funded visibility remains useful without turning into ad spam.
