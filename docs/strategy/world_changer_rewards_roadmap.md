# World Changer Rewards System

## Purpose

This document evaluates the proposed Handprint rewards system: using earned points, badges, affirmations, and shareable status to redirect familiar social-media reward mechanics toward verified real-world contribution.

## Working Thesis

Handprint should not eliminate dopamine. It should make the reward loop harder to fake, less extractive, and tied to real contribution. The core mechanic is:

Participation -> verification -> Handprint growth -> earned affirmation power -> stronger reputation -> more invitations to useful action.

## Recommended Expertise Roles

### Behavioral Design Lead

Design reward timing, feedback, progress, and habit loops without encouraging compulsive scrolling or empty engagement.

### Civic Trust And Safety Lead

Protect the system from vanity scoring, World Enabler favoritism, political weaponization, fake attendance, and popularity contests.

### Game Economy Designer

Balance points, badges, levels, leaderboards, scarcity, rewards, anti-farming rules, decay, and progression.

### Youth And Wellbeing Advisor

Evaluate whether the product creates healthy motivation, especially for teens, students, volunteer groups, and early-career users.

### World Enabler Operations Lead

Ensure event World Enablers can issue points and badges without creating too much admin work or inconsistent reward inflation.

### Credentialing And Partnerships Lead

Evaluate external training credentials such as first aid, CPR, lifeguarding, CERT, food handling, tutoring, or crisis-response training.

### Reputation Systems Architect

Design how World Changer status can become meaningful without becoming a brittle public score.

### Resume And Opportunity Design Advisor

Translate Handprint activity into useful proof for college applications, internships, jobs, scholarships, civic leadership, and nonprofit roles.

## Concept Assessment

The idea is strategically strong because it deepens Handprint's core differentiation: rewarding verified contribution rather than attention. It also gives the Handprint profile more status gravity. A profile becomes not just a record of participation, but a reputation object: what this person has done, what others trust them to do, and what they are ready to lead next.

The strongest product phrase is "earned affirmation." Likes, hearts, boosts, follows, and endorsements should not be free infinite taps. They should be limited expressions of recognition that users earn by doing real things.

The phrase "World Changer" is approved product language. It should be bold and emotionally legible, while staying grounded in verified contribution so it feels earned rather than inflated.

## Main Design Recommendation

Separate the system into four layers:

1. Contribution points: earned from verified participation.
2. Badges: symbolic proof of specific milestones or skills.
3. Affirmation credits: limited social recognition users can give to others.
4. World Changer level: a public status tier based on durable contribution patterns, not raw points alone.

Do not make one universal score do everything. A single score will be too easy to game and too socially reductive.

## Points Versus Badges

Points should measure contribution value inside the app economy. They can unlock levels, affirmation credits, and eligibility for leadership roles.

Badges should mostly be decorative and evidentiary. A badge should say, "I completed this type of thing," not "I am better than you."

Recommended rule: badges can carry metadata, but they should not be the main currency. Some verified badges can contribute to level eligibility, but the visual badge itself should remain a proof object.

## Event Point Allocation

Handprint should not let World Enablers freely decide any point value they want. That creates inflation and favoritism.

Use a guided point rubric:

- Base participation: small reward for showing up.
- Duration: modest increase for longer events.
- Skill intensity: increase for trained or specialized work.
- Responsibility: increase for leading, coordinating, mentoring, or post-event follow-up.
- Verification strength: higher confidence for World Enabler-confirmed completion than self-check-in.
- Community need: limited multiplier for urgent or hard-to-fill actions.
- Repeat reliability: bonus for consistent attendance over time, not raw volume alone.

World Enabler input should describe the event. Handprint should calculate the recommended point range. Admin/trust review can override in pilots.

## Affirmation Credits

Likes/hearts should become earned social energy.

Users earn affirmation credits through:

- Verified attendance.
- World Enabler confirmation.
- Completing training.
- Recruiting someone who attends.
- Helping a World Enabler fill a hard role.
- Receiving high-trust appreciation from verified World Enablers or peers.

Users spend affirmation credits to:

- Thank another user.
- Boost an event.
- Recognize a World Enabler.
- Endorse a teammate's contribution.
- Signal "I would show up with this person again."

Affirmation credits should refresh slowly and remain limited. This makes recognition meaningful.

## World Changer Status

Use bold tiers, but do not rank people against one another in V1.

Approved V1 tier model:

- Starter: first verified mark.
- Neighbor: repeated local participation.
- Helper: reliable contributor across multiple actions.
- Builder: contributes skills, recruits others, or supports World Enablers.
- Anchor: trusted by World Enablers and consistently confirmed.
- World Changer: exceptional sustained contribution with breadth, trust, and leadership.

Avoid showing exact ranking against other people in V1. Show progress against personal milestones and community contribution.

## Progression Tone

The progression system should feel game-like enough to be exciting:

- Big level name.
- Clear point progress.
- Badge wall.
- Earned social power.
- Reward reveal after check-in.
- Shareable status language.

But it should not feel like a casino, infinite feed, or popularity race. The emotional note is: "You did something real. The app noticed. Other people can now trust and join you."

## Healthy Dopamine Principles

- Reward action, not scrolling.
- Reward completion more than intention.
- Reward consistency without punishing rest.
- Reward helping others succeed, not just individual accumulation.
- Make time away from the app part of the loop.
- Use surprise sparingly and ethically.
- Avoid infinite feeds and public popularity leaderboards in V1.
- Provide "quiet pride" share artifacts instead of constant notifications.

## Referral And Recruitment

Referrals are powerful but dangerous. They can create spam, pressure, and low-quality attendance.

Recommended model:

- Recruiter gets credit only when the referred user attends or is World Enabler-confirmed.
- The referred user must understand what they are joining.
- Cap referral points per event.
- Give more value for recruiting into hard-to-fill roles than broad invite blasts.
- Track World Enabler feedback on referred attendees.

## Training And Credentials

Training should be a major long-term differentiator.

Examples:

- First aid / CPR.
- Lifeguard certification.
- Food handling.
- CERT or local emergency preparedness.
- Youth mentoring clearance.
- Tutoring training.
- Conflict de-escalation.
- Accessibility support.
- Translation or interpretation readiness.
- Event captain / volunteer lead training.

Credentials should be verified at different confidence levels:

- Self-declared.
- World Enabler-attested.
- Document-verified.
- Partner-issued.

Only higher-confidence credentials should affect leadership eligibility.

## Resume And Opportunity Value

The system should eventually generate a "Civic Contribution Record" that users can share for:

- Job applications.
- College applications.
- Scholarships.
- Internships.
- Service requirements.
- Leadership programs.
- World Enabler recruitment.

This record should emphasize verified actions, skills demonstrated, roles held, and trusted World Enabler attestations. It should not look like a gamified scorecard.

## Key Risks

### Moral Vanity

Users may perform goodness for status instead of contribution. Mitigation: emphasize verified usefulness and World Enabler/community feedback.

### Point Inflation

World Enablers may overvalue their own events. Mitigation: rubric-based point ranges and admin review.

### Popularity Capture

Charismatic users may earn more affirmation than quiet reliable users. Mitigation: separate World Enabler confirmation from peer applause.

### Social Pressure

Users may feel guilty for not constantly contributing. Mitigation: normalize seasons of involvement and do not punish rest.

### Gaming And Fraud

Users may fake attendance or referrals. Mitigation: World Enabler confirmation, geofenced check-in later, caps, anomaly detection, and audit trails.

### Ideological Weaponization

Points could imply endorsement of contested causes. Mitigation: neutral category framing, transparent trust review, and no campaign activity before legal review.

## Product Roadmap

### Phase 1: Concept Model

- Define points, badges, affirmation credits, and status tiers.
- Create reward rubric for event participation.
- Define badge types and verification levels.
- Add UX copy principles for healthy motivation.

### Phase 2: Prototype

- Add points to actions and completed marks.
- Add World Changer tier to the public profile.
- Add earned affirmation credits as a read-only preview.
- Add World Enabler confirmation reward messaging.
- Add one sample training badge.

### Phase 3: Pilot

- Let trusted World Enablers confirm attendance and issue standard badges.
- Keep point values admin-controlled.
- Test whether users understand and value the system.
- Track whether rewards increase attendance without creating spam.

### Phase 4: Social Layer

- Let users spend earned credits to recognize people and organizations.
- Add private appreciation before public likes.
- Add referral credit only after confirmed attendance.
- Add World Enabler-visible reliability signals.

### Phase 5: Credential Layer

- Add partner-issued training badges.
- Add evidence fields and verification confidence.
- Add leadership eligibility signals.
- Generate a shareable civic contribution record.

## Recommended V1 Decision

Implement a small, principled version first:

- Contribution points exist.
- Badges exist.
- World Changer tier exists.
- Likes/hearts are renamed into earned appreciation credits.
- Public leaderboard does not exist.
- World Enabler point values are rubric-controlled.
- Training credentials are modeled but not fully verified yet.

This keeps the reward loop motivational while avoiding the most obvious social-media traps.

## Achievement Pages, Not Diploma Bureaucracy

Badge detail pages should feel like praise pages with evidence underneath, not printable diplomas. The digital page is the canonical record for V1.

Founder decisions:

- Do not prioritize printable or downloadable certificate PDFs for V1.
- Do not add a QR code to every certificate or badge page.
- Use one QR code per user, pointing to the public World Changer page.
- Badge and achievement icons should link to a detail page with affirmation, World Enabler identity, contribution summary, Handprint points, and validation evidence.
- Use the user's official profile name on achievement pages.
- If a user changes their name later, achievements remain attached to the account and travel with them through life.
- Achievements do not expire and should not be revoked. Under the do-ism philosophy, completed good cannot be taken away after the fact.
- Training credentials can still require renewal when the underlying real-world credential expires, but that should not erase the historical achievement of having completed the training.
- Badge visibility should default to public. Users can hide an achievement, but that control should be quiet and secondary.

Implementation guidance: preserve immutable account and achievement IDs behind the scenes, but do not make serial numbers or verification bureaucracy the emotional center of the page.

## In-App Sharing First

Handprint should keep social sharing verifiable by default.

V1 sharing priority:

1. In-app sharing and appreciation remain the primary loop.
2. External sharing uses a link to the World Changer page, not exported image files.
3. Share messages can be bespoke, for example: "I just hit a World Changer milestone on Handprint. Come see what changed and join the next action."
4. External platforms should receive a link preview that routes back to the live Handprint page.

Do not make PNG badge exports a V1 priority. They can become screenshots, lose context, and weaken verification.

## Reach Rewards

Real-world rewards are approved for the public pilot if they are controlled by funding, sponsorship, and availability toggles. The product framing should be "expand your reach," not "cash out points."

Reward categories:

- Recognition: badges, achievement pages, World Changer tiers, World Enabler affirmations.
- Capacity: training scholarships, course fees, equipment, transportation, supplies.
- Aspirational: travel fellowships, ambassador opportunities, major sponsor awards.
- Sponsor: branded rewards that expand user capacity without letting sponsors define impact.
- Community-funded: microgrants or local support funded by event registration surplus, donors, or sponsors.

Founder decisions:

- Keep "Goody Two Shoes Award" as the public reward name and reclaim it as a positive phrase.
- Tie the shoe reward to impact that involved walking, delivery, cleanup, canvassing, or other real mileage so the humor fits.
- Include real-world rewards in the public pilot, provided the app has a monetary strategy through event registration fees, sponsorships, or other funding pools.
- Build rewards so each can be limited, paused, or turned off by market, funding, sponsor, age group, or risk review.

High-value rewards should require verified impact plus human review. Raw points alone should unlock eligibility, not automatically trigger expensive prizes.

## Implementation Decision From Founder Review

- "World Changer" is the final status language for the prototype.
- The first prototype should use a bold progression treatment, not a subtle reputation-only treatment.
- The reward layer should now appear in Discover, post-event check-in, the Wave tab, and the public Handprint profile.
- Achievement details should remain digital, verifiable, celebratory, and simple.
- Real-world Reach Rewards belong in the public pilot with sponsor/funding controls.
