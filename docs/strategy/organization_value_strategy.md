# World Enabler Value Strategy

## Purpose

Handprint must benefit World Enablers and their organizations as clearly as it benefits individual World Changers. World Enabler supply is the product's oxygen: if hosts do not return, users will not find enough useful actions to make their Handprint grow.

## Core Value Proposition

Handprint helps World Enablers:

1. Fill useful roles with interested, local participants.
2. Convert event work into a verified mobilization record.
3. Earn trust-building accolades that can support grants, donors, sponsors, and community credibility.
4. Raise funds or promote opportunities without turning the product into an ad feed.
5. Show donors what was accomplished through plain-language impact receipts.

## V1 Default Rule

New Handprint product ideas should be assumed into the V1 launch plan unless there is a strong product, trust, legal, safety, or operational reason to hold them back.

## World Enabler Handprint

World Enablers should have their own public-facing Handprint over time. This should not be a vanity score. It should be a verified contribution record showing:

- Events hosted.
- Attendees mobilized.
- Confirmed participants.
- Volunteer hours.
- Beneficiaries served.
- Impact receipts.
- World Enabler accolades earned.
- Repeat event reliability.
- Sponsor and fundraising disclosures.

This gives World Enablers a useful artifact for grant applications, annual reports, donor updates, recruitment, and community accountability.

## World Enabler Accolades

World Enabler accolades should recognize repeatable, evidence-backed behavior.

Recommended categories:

- Mobilization: consistently brings people into useful action.
- Impact: completes concrete work with a named beneficiary.
- Fundraising: connects donation asks to verified outcomes.
- Trust: confirms attendance, protects privacy, and avoids misleading claims.
- Sponsor Stewardship: uses sponsor visibility without buying false legitimacy.

These accolades should feel celebratory, but they must be harder to earn than ordinary marketing badges.

## Impact-First Fundraising

Fundraising can belong in Handprint if it is specific, plain-language, and tied to what the event accomplishes.

Required fields for fundraising listings:

- Beneficiary.
- Intended use of funds.
- Amount being raised.
- Post-event or post-campaign impact receipt plan.
- Receipt, delivery, or beneficiary attestation plan when practical.

Financial transparency should be revisited later as an optional add-on. V1 should not foreground overhead, funds-to-impact ratios, or financial dashboards in the app.

## Sponsored Visibility

Sponsored listings can be useful if they are controlled and elegant.

V1 rules:

- Sponsored visibility never buys Handprint points.
- Sponsored visibility never buys World Enabler accolades.
- Sponsored posts must be labeled plainly.
- Users must be able to mark sponsored or awareness posts as not interested.
- Targeting should use user-declared interests and location, not sensitive inferred traits.
- Sponsored placements should be limited in density.
- Sponsored campaigns should be tied to opportunities, fundraising, training, or service, not generic brand impressions.

The product standard is: sponsor dollars should expand the reach of real action, not rent the user's attention.

## Grant-Ready Reporting

World Enablers should eventually be able to export or share a grant-ready Handprint report showing:

- Mobilization metrics.
- Attendance confirmation rate.
- Volunteer-hour totals.
- Event categories and populations served.
- Impact evidence.
- Impact receipts.
- Repeat World Enabler reliability.
- Sponsor/funder contributions.

V1 can model this as an on-page report preview before export exists.

## Risks

### Ad Feed Drift

If sponsored placement becomes too easy or too frequent, Handprint starts feeling like Facebook.

Mitigation: limit sponsored density, prioritize user-declared interests, and separate ads from rewards.

### Pay-To-Legitimacy

Sponsors or organizations may try to buy trust.

Mitigation: never sell points, verification, accolades, or trust tier.

### Fundraising Misrepresentation

World Enablers may overstate impact.

Mitigation: require beneficiary, intended use, impact receipt plan, and post-campaign accomplishment evidence.

### Privacy Leakage

World Enabler impact reports can accidentally expose sensitive participant or beneficiary information.

Mitigation: report aggregate outcomes, not private client details.

## V1 Recommendation

Add a World Enabler Handprint preview to the World Enabler tab:

- Two sample World Enabler profiles.
- Mobilization metrics.
- Impact proof.
- Grant-ready proof summary.
- Accolade examples.
- Sponsored visibility rule.
- Public World Enabler pages.
- Accolade evidence pages.
- Impact receipt pages.
- Sponsor density limits.
- Admin controls for World Enabler accolade approval.

Do not add payment processing, ad buying, or exportable reports yet. The pilot should validate whether World Enablers understand and want the reputation/reporting layer first.

## Later Optional Add-On

Financial transparency can later become an official add-on with clearer links to external financials, annual reports, audited statements, or campaign accounting. It should not be part of the default V1 app surface.
