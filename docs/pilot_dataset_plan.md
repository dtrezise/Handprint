# Pilot Dataset Plan

The app should launch in one dense community with enough verified actions to make the first session feel real.

## Default Pilot

Name: Northside pilot

Purpose: prove that local action discovery plus a visible personal Handprint motivates first RSVP, attendance, and sharing.

## Seed Targets

- 10-20 verified organizers
- 50-100 actions in the first month
- 12-18 actions visible in week one
- 5-7 categories represented
- 3 anchor partners with recurring actions
- 1 manual support owner

## Starter Organizer Types

1. Food pantry or community fridge
2. Library civic help desk
3. Neighborhood cleanup group
4. Arts/community studio
5. Mutual aid group
6. Campus service office
7. Faith/community service group
8. Tenant or civic education organization
9. Preparedness or resilience group
10. Youth mentoring group after extra review

## Required Fields Per Action

- title
- organizer
- contact email
- category
- summary
- location name
- neighborhood
- approximate distance
- starts at
- duration
- capacity
- skills needed
- expected impact
- safety note
- review note
- trust tier
- status

## Week-One Mix

- 3 food support actions
- 2 cleanup actions
- 2 civic forum/listening actions
- 2 mutual aid/admin support actions
- 2 arts/community actions
- 1 preparedness action
- 1 mentoring action, escalated until youth policy is complete

## Data Quality Rules

- Every action needs a human-readable reason to join.
- Every action needs a safety note, even if low-risk.
- No open campaign, candidate, fundraising, medical, or legal-claims actions without legal review.
- Youth-facing actions stay escalated until background-check and supervision policies are documented.
- Organizers should receive quick feedback after every submitted action.
