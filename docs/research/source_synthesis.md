# Source Synthesis

This synthesis is based on the three foundation PDFs supplied for the project.

## Core Concept

Handprint reframes impact away from guilt and toward agency. The central metaphor is that each person can affect the world "within the stretch of my arms."

## Strongest Insight

The handprint itself is the strongest differentiator. It turns verified participation into visible identity capital. That gives the product a reason to exist beyond event discovery.

## Product Components From Source Material

- Profile layer: interests, values, causes, geography, skills, availability, engagement level.
- Sphere of influence engine: proximity, social graph, alignment, skills, overlap.
- Event marketplace: civic/community actions instead of dating-style people swipes.
- Organizer layer: event creation, promotion, analytics, targeting.
- Handprint visualization: participation creates lines, texture, depth, and personal story.

## Main Risks From Source Material

- Neutrality at scale is hard.
- The product could become polarized.
- Cold start is severe.
- Moderation costs could become large.
- Political mobilization creates legal and reputational risk.
- Incumbents already own pieces of the behavior.

## Strategic Interpretation

Handprint should not initially compete as a broad marketplace. It should prove a narrow but emotionally distinctive loop:

1. Find something useful near me.
2. Participate.
3. See my handprint grow.
4. Return because my civic identity now has continuity.
